# Garrido fidelity audit — causal and held-out verdict

Date: 2026-07-10  
Primary evidence: Garrido-Rios (2017), `Raw_data1+Re.xlsx`,
`Raw_data2+Re.xlsx`; `Rsult_1.xlsx` is secondary aggregate evidence.

## Corrected executive verdict

The repository is a **high-fidelity transcription of Garrido's static
specification**, but it is **not yet a validated endogenous reproduction of the
original experiments**.  The phrase “the core DES faithfully reproduces the
thesis” is therefore prohibited until the held-out gate passes.

Static reporters confirm the 13-operation topology, Table 6.16 buffers, Table
6.20 capacities, demand calendar, risk parameter tables and visible Excel ReT
formula.  The raw workbooks contain 47,546 usable order rows; formula
recomputation has zero mismatches.  These facts establish transcription and
metric fidelity, not queue/process fidelity.

## Critical causal defect found and repaired

Before this audit, Op1 contracting and Op2 supplier delivery were independent
clocks.  R12 could change order-level risk attribution and ReT while leaving
production, deliveries, demand and backlog identical.  A same-seed probe gave
identical physical totals for risks-off, R12 current/increased and R13
current/increased even though ReT ranged from 0.8983 to 0.1128.

The new opt-in `procurement_contract_mode="causal_coupled"`:

1. starts the first Op1 contract at t=0;
2. completes it after the thesis PT=672 h;
3. gives it an explicit validity window;
4. blocks Op2 whenever no valid contract exists;
5. makes forced R12/R13 events delay supplier-delivery times.

`legacy_independent` remains the default solely to reproduce existing Track B
checkpoints.  Mixing a legacy checkpoint with the repaired environment is
forbidden.

## Causal liveness and conservation gates

`tests/test_garrido_causal_fidelity_gate.py` forces each risk against a
same-seed no-risk counterfactual.  All ten tests pass:

- R12 delays contract completion and supplier delivery;
- R13 delays supplier delivery;
- R11, R21 and R3 change assembly output;
- R22/R23 change the downstream delivery schedule;
- R24 changes demand without changing unrelated production capacity;
- R14 changes good output and enters rework;
- affected-operation ledgers match the forced contract;
- raw-material and ration conservation residuals are zero.

The engine now distinguishes `total_theatre_inflow` from
`total_order_fulfilled`; legacy `total_delivered` remains an alias for theatre
inflow.

## Finite calibration and untouched validation

Runner: `scripts/gate_garrido_reference_v2.py`  
Artifact: `outputs/audits/garrido_reference_v2_gate/gate.json`

Calibration uses only odd CFs. Even CFs are untouched validation cases. No
Excel order or risk tape is replayed inside the DES.

Global grid:

- raw-material multiplier: 1.0, 1.5, 2.0;
- ledger delay: 0, 48.00744, 54 h.

One permitted refinement at the selected multiplier tested 72, 96 and 120 h.
The best artificial-lag calibration cell was multiplier 1.0 and delay 120 h.
The fact that a 120 h artificial delay beats the thesis-derived 48 h value is
evidence that the old ledger delay was absorbing missing order/queue physics.
It is not a parameter to promote.

The eligible candidate therefore replaces that delay entirely:

- demand begins after the Op9 warm-up trigger;
- each order reserves available stock at Op9;
- the reserved quantity traverses Op10 and Op12;
- R22/R23 can extend its actual delivery time;
- CTj is generated by transport and queue timing.
- RPj follows the thesis Algorithm 2 elapsed-time interpretation rather than
  the legacy overlap-sum interpretation.

This mechanism improves CT medians substantially: held-out medians are mostly
78–118 h versus workbook medians around 99–126 h. It still understates the
upper tails and recovery periods, so promotion remains blocked.

### Held-out results

| Gate | Result | Pass? |
|---|---:|:---:|

The previous multiplier 2.0 is rejected by this finite grid.  The structural
repair improves causal validity, but it does not close the distributional gap.

## Environment separation

- `supply_chain/data/garrido_reference_v2_candidate_2026-07-10.json` is frozen
  as **blocked, not promoted**. New RL training is prohibited.
- `supply_chain/data/adaptive_research_v1_freeze_2026-07-10.json` freezes the
  Garrido-informed research extension separately. Existing checkpoints may be
  evaluated only under legacy physics; a repaired adaptive environment will
  require retraining and a new static frontier.

## Remaining blocker

The next blocker is no longer the fixed fulfillment delay; that replacement is
implemented. The remaining mismatch is queue/recovery physics: CT p95 is
generally too short; elapsed RP fixes much of R2 but makes the R1 upper tail
roughly twice the workbook in several CFs. The next audit must reconstruct the
family-specific event-to-order attribution for RPj/DPj and determine which
serial queues or release rules are absent. Do not tune more constants against
the validation CFs.

## Claim language

> validation gates.

Not allowed now:

`adaptive_benchmark_v2` remains a deliberate Garrido-informed extension: all
risks may coexist, regimes are non-stationary and its horizon/action contract

non-comparability, but it does not excuse the failed endogenous gate.
