Reward Design

Equation 3 — Cobb–Douglas resilience reward (ReT_seq_v1)
$$r_t = \text{SC}_t^{\,0.60}\;\times\;\text{BC}_t^{\,0.25}\;\times\;\text{AE}_t^{\,0.15} - \kappa$$
$\text{SC}_t$
Service continuity — maps to Garrido Eq. 5.4 (fill rate component)
$\text{BC}_t$
Backorder control — proxy for Eq. 5.2 (recovery speed)
$\text{AE}_t$
Adaptive efficiency — extends Sec. 8.6.2 (shift-cost penalty)
$\kappa$
Baseline offset = 0.20 (ensures near-static policies earn ≈ 0)

Exponents reflect military service hierarchy: service (0.60) >> recovery (0.25) >> cost (0.15). The multiplicative Cobb–Douglas form ensures non-compensability: any factor approaching 0 collapses the entire reward.

Equation 4 — Thesis piecewise resilience metric ReT (Garrido 2017, Eq. 5.5)
$$\text{Re}^T = \begin{cases} \overline{\text{Re}}^{\,\max}\!\left(\dfrac{\text{AP}_j}{\text{LT}}\right) & \text{Autotomy period (AP}_j\text{)} \\[8pt] \overline{\text{Re}}\!\left(\dfrac{1}{\text{RP}_j}\right) & \text{Recovery period (RP}_j\text{)} \\[8pt] \overline{\text{Re}}^{\,\min}\!\left(\dfrac{\text{DP}_j - \text{RP}_j}{\text{CT}_j}\right) & \text{Disruption period (DP}_j - \text{RP}_j\text{)} \\[8pt] 1 - \dfrac{B_t + U_t}{D_t} & \text{Non-disruption period (N-DP}_j\text{)} \end{cases}$$

This piecewise form is used for audit only, not as training reward. When used as RL objective, discontinuities cause S1 collapse (99.9% S=1 convergence).

Equation 5 — PPO clipped objective
$$L^{\text{CLIP}}(\theta) = \hat{\mathbb{E}}_t\!\left[\min\!\Big(r_t(\theta)\,\hat{A}_t,\;\text{clip}\big(r_t(\theta),\,1-\epsilon,\,1+\epsilon\big)\,\hat{A}_t\Big)\right]$$
$r_t(\theta)$
probability ratio $\pi_\theta(a_t|s_t) / \pi_{\theta_{\text{old}}}(a_t|s_t)$
$\hat{A}_t$
GAE advantage estimate ($\lambda = 0.95$)
$\epsilon$
clip range = 0.2

Contribution Claims

C1 (Empirical–Negative): Under the thesis-faithful action contract, no RL configuration (across 14 reward modes, 5 observation versions, 2 algorithms including RecurrentPPO) outperforms static policies.

C2 (Empirical–Positive): A minimal action-space extension adding downstream distribution control produces fill rate = 1.000, +3.4 pp versus s2_d1.00, +1.2 pp versus the best static policy, and +47% reward versus the best static policy.

C3 (Structural diagnosis): RL effectiveness for SCRES depends on action-space-to-bottleneck alignment. The benchmark brings together bottleneck reasoning, RL control design, and action-contract selection in one empirically auditable setting.

C4 (Methodological): The benchmark operationalizes a Cobb–Douglas resilience function as part of the RL reward and reporting pipeline, extending Garrido et al. (2024) into an auditable control benchmark.
