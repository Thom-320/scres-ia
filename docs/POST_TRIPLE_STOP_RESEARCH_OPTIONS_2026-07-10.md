# Research options after the triple stop

**Decision status:** preserve Option A as the primary publication route; develop
Option B only as a separately preregistered research lane. Do not alter the
terminal claims of Track B, Track C, or Program L v1.

## Option A — The paper already supported by the evidence

The three negative programs form one publishable methodological result:

> **When not to use RL: preregistered gates for DES–RL resilience studies.**

The corpus combines same-contract frontiers, oracle gates, exact-replay
branching, persistent/reset/frozen identification controls, and explicit
non-identifiability boundaries. Together they show that an apparent learned
advantage can disappear when comparator decision rights are aligned and that
training is unwarranted when the tested contract exposes no deployable dynamic
headroom.

The remaining work is editorial consolidation, not additional experiments.
Three overlapping manuscript skeletons — the Track B C&IE pivot, the Track C
extension, and the Program L boundary — must become one paper.

The 18-policy panel and resource frontier also address the thesis's open
questions on inventory–capacity interaction (§8.6.1) and resource-aware
resilience optimization (§8.6.2) using classical policies; RL need not win for
those managerial findings to matter.

## Option B — Program v2: a deliberate change of decision class

If adaptive headroom is pursued again, it must live in a new physical and
decision contract:

1. Emergency reserve downstream of the threatened arc (Op11/theatre), with a
   real replenishment lead time and no replenishment through a failed route.
2. An explicit imperfect 14-day warning channel with 20% false positives and
   20% false negatives, plus a shuffled-alert placebo.
3. Finite, costly emergency capacity so scarcity resembles B0 — the only v1
   setting with action heterogeneity — rather than allowing every actuator to
   remain maximal.
4. A sub-weekly dispatch decision epoch.

Program v2 reuses the Program L environment discipline, exact tapes, branching
gate, fixed normalizers, and retained/reset/frozen harness. It does not reuse
the v1 promotion result. The prior probability of a learned advantage is low:
three decision classes measured headroom ceilings at or below a few percent.
Therefore v2 requires a new preregistration and a terminal pre-training gate.

## Update: preventive v2/v3 execution

The proposed downstream-reserve class was implemented and evaluated without
training PPO. v2 stopped under a fixed 336 h replenishment lead. A separately
preregistered v3 corrected the physical path to Op10 24 h + Op11 availability +
Op12 24 h and used 30 new paired tapes. v3 also stopped:

- the reserve mechanism was live on 30/30 tapes;
- perfect warning improved service 17.86% over zero reserve;
- imperfect warning improved only 1.18% over a shuffled placebo, with CI95
  crossing zero;
- static 15k captured the resource-efficient value.

Therefore Option B is terminal for this reserve-and-warning class. It
strengthens Option A: the physical redesign creates reserve value, but not
sufficient adaptive information value to justify RL.
