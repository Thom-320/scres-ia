## Revised scientific target

Garrido's stated publication criterion is a dynamic policy that beats a static
policy in resilience. Therefore `ret_excel_visible_v1` is the sole primary
endpoint. `ret_excel_visible_clipped_0_1` is a mandatory sensitivity. Service
loss, worst-CSSU performance, lost orders, backlog and conservation are binding
non-catastrophic-harm guardrails, not co-primary improvement requirements.

## Revised promotion rule

The observable spatial-allocation tree promotes only if all hold on held-out
calibration and again on the single virgin opening:

1. paired ReT delta versus the frozen best constant has CI95 lower bound above
   zero and is positive on at least 70% of tapes;
2. the clipped sensitivity has CI95 lower bound above zero;
3. the tree captures at least 50% of cross-fitted oracle ReT headroom;
4. upper CI95 relative degradation is at most 2% for lost orders, service-loss
   AUC and backlog AUC;
5. worst-CSSU ReT does not fall by more than 0.01 unless mission priority was
   fixed prospectively;
6. mass and total transport capacity are conserved;
7. the advantage occurs under true localization and does not reproduce under
   shuffled localization.

A pass supports observable, state-contingent spatial allocation that improves
Garrido ReT relative to a static allocation. It does not by itself establish
retained learning, prevention, anticipation, or organizational learning.
