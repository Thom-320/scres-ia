\title{When Does Reinforcement Learning Improve Supply Chain Resilience? Action-Space Alignment in a Garrido-Grounded DES Benchmark}
\begin{abstract}
Supply chain resilience studies increasingly combine simulation and reinforcement learning, yet it remains unclear when neural control improves resilience beyond strong static baselines. We develop a Garrido-grounded discrete-event simulation benchmark of a 13-operation military food supply chain and evaluate learned policies under dense static frontiers, common random numbers, and order-level Garrido/Excel resilience metrics. Under Track A, which preserves the original buffer/shift decision family, dense-frontier evaluation reveals no publishable dynamic-control advantage: learned policies fail to convert the small measured headroom over the best static policy. Under Track B, a DES-preserving operational extension that adds downstream dispatch control at Op10 and Op12, PPO improves Excel ReT over the best of 147 dense static dispatch comparators by +0.000426, with a fully positive 95\% confidence interval. The gain corresponds to roughly six percentage points of the attainable metric ceiling and is accompanied by large improvements in flow fill, service-loss AUC, backlog, and CTj/RPj/DPj recovery tails; a dispatch-inclusive cost sensitivity shows the policy also becomes cheaper in total than the best static once downstream expediting carries a moderate positive charge. Action-space ablations show downstream dispatch access is sufficient to recover the largest observed advantage, ruling out action-space size as the explanation. Two privileged-observation tests show that the result does not depend on oracle regime information: PPO retains most of its advantage when regime and forecast fields are masked, while a non-learning regime-conditioned lookup table fails to approach PPO. A cross-regime stress evaluation shows positive gains with fully positive seed-clustered confidence intervals in current and increased risk regimes at both horizons, with the severe service-floor regime as the disclosed boundary. The contribution is not architectural novelty, but a benchmark-centered diagnosis: reinforcement learning improves SCRES when the controllable interface reaches the binding operational bottleneck and the resulting headroom is dynamically convertible into recovery performance.
\end{abstract}

\section{Comparator-selection robustness}\label{appendix-robustness}

Because the headline comparator (the strongest of 147 statics) is
selected on the same evaluation episodes used to score PPO, comparator
selection could in principle inflate the reported delta (a
winner's-curse concern). Table~\ref{tab:top12_robustness} recomputes the
canonical paired delta against each of the twelve strongest static
comparators individually. All twelve deltas are positive with confidence
intervals excluding zero, and the delta \emph{grows} down the ranking ---
the opposite of the pattern selection bias would produce. Comparator
selection cannot flip the sign of the result.
