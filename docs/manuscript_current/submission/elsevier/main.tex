\title{When Does Reinforcement Learning Improve Supply Chain Resilience? Action-Space Alignment in a Garrido-Grounded DES Benchmark}
\begin{abstract}
Supply chain resilience studies increasingly combine simulation and reinforcement learning, yet it remains unclear when neural control improves resilience beyond strong static baselines. We develop a Garrido-grounded discrete-event simulation benchmark of a 13-operation military food supply chain and evaluate learned policies under dense static frontiers, common random numbers, and order-level Garrido/Excel resilience metrics. Under Track A, which preserves the original buffer/shift decision family, dense-frontier evaluation reveals no publishable dynamic-control advantage: learned policies fail to convert the small measured headroom over the best static policy. Under Track B, a DES-preserving operational extension that adds downstream dispatch control at Op10 and Op12, PPO improves Excel ReT over the best of 147 dense static dispatch comparators by +0.000426, with a fully positive 95\% confidence interval. The gain is accompanied by large improvements in flow fill, service-loss AUC, backlog, and CTj/RPj/DPj recovery tails, but not by a strict assembly-cost win. Action-space ablations show that the advantage concentrates in downstream dispatch access rather than in action-space size. Two privileged-observation tests show that the result does not depend on oracle regime information: PPO retains most of its advantage when regime and forecast fields are masked, while a non-learning regime-conditioned lookup table fails to approach PPO. Cross-regime evaluation shows positive gains in current and increased risk regimes at both horizons, with severe short-horizon disruption as a boundary case. The contribution is not architectural novelty, but a benchmark-centered diagnosis: reinforcement learning improves SCRES when the controllable interface reaches the binding operational bottleneck and the resulting headroom is dynamically convertible into recovery performance.
\end{abstract}
