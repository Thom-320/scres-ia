Dear Editor,

Please consider our manuscript, **“When Feedback Beats Every Static Policy but Not Structured Control: Exact Benchmarking of Recurrent RL in a Supply-Chain DES,”** for publication in *Computers & Industrial Engineering*.

The paper addresses a recurring identification problem in simulation-based industrial engineering: a learned controller may outperform a few fixed policies without showing whether the benefit comes from feedback or from specifically neural capability. We construct a multiproduct extension of a thesis-grounded supply-chain discrete-event simulation in which all 65,536 admissible eight-week open-loop calendars can be enumerated. Ten frozen RecurrentPPO policies, evaluated prospectively on 256 common-random-number tapes in each of three cells, outperform the complete static frontier. The same policies are practically equivalent, not superior, to a strong belief-based controller, and do not establish the frozen worst-product service margin.

The contribution is therefore not a new reinforcement-learning architecture. It is a reproducible industrial-engineering methodology that separates static-policy discovery, feedback value, neural premium, and service safety. The repository includes an exact replay of the source Excel resilience formula, explicit comparator rights, simultaneous inference with comparator reselection, a direct full-DES audit, and one pipeline for every submitted table and figure.
