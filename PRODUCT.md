# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The primary users are IJPR/IJPE/COR reviewers, editors, and supply-chain
resilience researchers reading a manuscript, supplement, or Word/Overleaf port.
They arrive skeptical, time-constrained, and trained to look for overclaiming,
weak baselines, pseudo-replication, and citation gaps. Secondary users are the
authors and collaborators who need a stable design and evidence contract while
rewriting the paper across LaTeX, Word Online, and reviewer-response artifacts.

## Product Purpose

This project produces a Q1-ready scholarly manuscript and evidence package for
a Garrido-grounded military food supply-chain DES benchmark. Success means the
paper communicates a narrow, defensible result: PPO improves adaptive recovery
in the designed Track B bottleneck-aligned cell; the claim survives static,
heuristic, regime-table, masked-observation, ablation, and cross-regime checks;
and every number traces to the final evidence bundle.

## Brand Personality

Rigorous, restrained, and forensic. The artifact should feel like a careful
engineering paper with a strong diagnostic argument: precise enough for
reviewers, sober enough to avoid hype, and visually clear enough that the
evidence hierarchy is visible before the reader studies every table.

## Anti-references

Do not look like a generic AI/RL hype paper, SaaS pitch deck, dark-mode
dashboard, consultancy slide, or over-designed magazine spread. Avoid perfect
fill, 57% cost-win, universal RL-law, anticipation, retained-learning, and
unclosed generalization language. Avoid decorative gradients, nested cards,
purple/blue AI palettes, vague hero-metric visuals, and figure captions that
make claims the evidence bundle does not support.

## Design Principles

1. Evidence before flourish: visual hierarchy must guide readers from claim to
   artifact path to uncertainty boundary.
2. Thesis lineage remains visible: topology, ReT, and validation figures should
   visibly connect the Python benchmark back to Garrido-Rios and the original
   workbooks.
3. Strong claim, narrow boundary: figures should make the Track A/Track B
   contrast and severe-stress boundary legible without turning them into a
   universal RL slogan.
4. Reviewer defensibility: every table and graphic should be citeable in a
   response letter with exact metric names, comparator families, and caveats.
5. Portable craft: LaTeX is the source of truth, but figures and tables must
   survive export to Word Online and Overleaf without becoming blurry,
   inconsistent, or visually noisy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use colorblind-safe palettes, redundant labels rather than color-only encoding,
legible figure text at journal scale, and high-contrast table/figure annotation.
Avoid dense decorative backgrounds that damage print readability. Motion is not
part of the manuscript surface. The Word/Overleaf port must preserve alt-text
intent, figure captions, and evidence traceability.
