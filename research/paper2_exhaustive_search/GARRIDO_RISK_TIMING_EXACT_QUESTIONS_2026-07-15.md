# Garrido exact questions — recurrent-risk stress and intervention timing

These questions determine the operational claim boundary of the recurrent-risk and
endogenous-timing lane. They do not ask for endorsement of a numerical result. A positive
answer requires units, timing, authority, plausible ranges, and a source record or explicit
signed expert judgment.

## RT1 — Meaning and admissible scope of the increased-frequency cells

In Tables 6.12–6.15 and configurations Cf1–Cf20, are the `current` and `increased`
occurrence levels intended as defensible military stress-test scenarios for the same MFSC,
or only as abstract experimental factors? Specifically for R22 and R24, which current and
increased inter-arrival ranges should be used, over what campaign duration, and may a paper
interpret them as peacetime versus high-tempo/war conditions? Are the 1.5× and 2× impact
cells operationally plausible sensitivities, or must they be labelled researcher stress
extensions?

**Adjudication:** If only the exact Cf frequency levels are supported, impact sensitivities
remain report-only researcher extensions. If even the increased frequency levels are not a
representative MFSC envelope, any positive remains a synthetic stress-test result.

## RT2 — Real decision authority and multiscale implementation delays

After observing operations, how often may MFSC management actually revise each decision:

- Op10/Op12 dispatch quantities or departure plans;
- assembly shifts S1/S2/S3;
- strategic inventory targets at Op3, Op5 and Op9?

For each, what is the observation-to-decision latency, activation delay, minimum dwell,
approval authority, cancellation/reassignment rule, and finite resource or budget? Is a
24-hour review opportunity plausible? Can shifts ramp by one level per day? Does a strategic
buffer change require approximately seven days and become irrevocable while pending?

**Adjudication:** Unsupported daily freedom narrows the claim to a researcher timing
extension. Supported daily dispatch but weekly buffer commitment licenses only the
multiscale action surface, not universal daily reoptimization.

## RT3 — What is observable before and after R22/R24?

For R22 and R24, what timestamped information is available to the decision maker before a
dispatch/shift/buffer commitment and after the event begins? Please specify signal source,
latency, false-positive/false-negative behavior, geographic/product scope, and whether the
signal is a forecast, an active-event report, or only downstream backlog/inventory effects.
Does any manager know an R22/R24 onset 24, 72 or 168 hours in advance?

**Adjudication:** Without pre-event information, negative offsets remain privileged ceiling
only. An observable controller may use only realized history and operational state. A
positive warning answer authorizes a separate observation contract; it does not retroactively
convert the privileged timing ceiling into `H_obs`.

## RT4 — Service trajectory and recovery evidence

Which operational record defines daily service performance during and after an R22/R24
cluster: complete requested ration quantity, issued quantity, unresolved/lost requests, or
another ledger? What baseline and sustained-recovery criterion is operationally meaningful?
Is recovery to at least 95% of the pre-event median for seven consecutive days defensible,
and is an eight-week post-cluster window adequate for the MFSC?

**Adjudication:** Until answered, service-loss AUC, maximum drop, and system recovery time
remain secondary diagnostics. Canonical request-snapshot ReT remains the only promotion
endpoint.

## RT5 — Correlation, overlap and locality of recurrent risk

May R22 route attacks and R24 contingent demand occur independently, or are they correlated
during the same campaign? When events overlap, which routes/nodes/products are jointly
affected, and does one event change the occurrence or impact distribution of the other?
Provide the plausible overlap/correlation envelope and whether the thesis's independent
streams were a deliberate simplification.

**Adjudication:** The current experiment retains independent per-risk CRN. Evidence of
correlation would license a new preregistered joint-tape sensitivity, never a post hoc rescue
of a failed independent-risk contract.
