## Q11 — Mission-expiry / admission triage (reopens R09)

**Context:** The thesis assumes late orders become backorders (never abandoned). R09 requires
hard deadlines + permanent abandonment + triage authority — a non-work-conserving admission
problem, materially distinct from D1 (closed).

**Question:** "For theatre requirements — especially R24 contingent-demand orders:
(a) Does an unfilled ration requirement have a HARD deadline after which it is PERMANENTLY
abandoned (mission moves/ends, rations no longer needed), rather than backordered indefinitely?
(b) If so, what is the deadline distribution, and is it TIGHTER than the 24–120 h R21/R23/R22
recovery timescales?
(c) Does the logistics agency hold doctrinal authority to triage/decline which orders enter the
fulfillment pipeline (admission control)?
(d) Do R24 orders carry mission-criticality CLASSES beyond the single contingent-priority flag?"

**Reopens R09 if:** hard deadlines tighter than recovery times exist AND triage authority exists.
**Closes R09 if:** orders always backorder, deadlines looser than recovery, or no triage authority
→ collapses to D1 (an expired-in-denominator order is metrically identical to a lost backorder on
canonical ReT).

## Q12 — APP cost ledger for the Cobb-Douglas factory-resilience lane (licenses or closes CD co-primary)

**Context:** The Garrido et al. 2024 factory-resilience index needs five variables (inventory,
backorders, spare capacity, time-to-fulfill, cost). The MFSC DES computes inventory/backorders/
time but its cost ledger OMITS hiring, firing, and overtime (the APP labor costs); the CD v1 gate
was rejected partly for this. Governance keeps CD secondary unless a validated APP ledger + a
sourced calibration + Garrido endorsement exist.

**Question:** "For the Op5–Op7 assembly plant:
(a) Is the workforce/shift level (S1/S2/S3) a genuine PERIOD-BY-PERIOD decision, with hiring,
firing, and overtime costs — or is the balanced-line workforce fixed?
(b) What are the RELATIVE cost coefficients (even approximate ratios) for regular production,
overtime, holding inventory, backorders, hiring, and firing?
(c) Is there installed-vs-used capacity (spare capacity) as a measurable line quantity?
(d) Would you ENDORSE applying your published factory-resilience index to the MFSC Op5–Op7
sub-system as a CO-PRIMARY construct alongside ReT, with re-calibration to MFSC variable ranges —
or is the index scoped only to the standalone factory model?"

**Reopens the CD-APP lane if:** workforce/overtime is a real decision with a validated cost ledger
AND Garrido endorses the MFSC mapping → CD can be computed faithfully; co-primary gate legitimate.
**Closes the CD-APP lane if:** fixed workforce with no hire/fire/overtime, OR Garrido scopes the
index to the standalone factory only → CD stays secondary construct-sensitivity (as governance
already holds); no co-primary.
